## Quick Answer

Yes, you can create a credible architectural competition entry in 20 minutes using only your phone. The formula: **2 minutes for concept lock → 6 minutes for AI hero image → 8 minutes for 3-board layout → 4 minutes for text polish.** Success depends on constraint exploitation, not detail perfection.

| Time | Task | Tool |
|------|------|------|
| 0-2 min | Concept lock | Brain only |
| 3-8 min | Hero image generation | Nano Banana / Midjourney mobile |
| 9-16 min | Board layout | Canva / Picsart (mobile) |
| 17-20 min | Text & submission | Notes app + email |

---

## The Reality Check

Most competition entries fail not from lack of detail, but from **conceptual confusion**. Jurors review hundreds of boards. Clarity wins over complexity every time.

**What a 20-minute entry CAN do:**
- Present one strong, legible concept
- Show a compelling hero image
- Communicate your core idea in 3 sentences

**What it CANNOT do:**
- Detailed technical drawings
- Elaborated structural systems
- Multiple design iterations

## Minute 0-2: Concept Lock (The Most Important 120 Seconds)

Don't sketch. Don't browse Pinterest. Answer three questions:

### Question 1: What's the site's most obvious feature?
| Feature Type | Design Response |
|-------------|----------------|
| Waterfront | Float / frame / filter the view |
| Dense urban | Carve void / vertical green / rooftop |
| Heritage adjacent | Contrast / echo / reveal layers |
| Natural landscape | Extend / dissolve / platform |

### Question 2: What value does the brief emphasize?
| Brief Priority | Your Angle |
|---------------|-----------|
| Sustainability | One measurable strategy (solar, rainwater, passive) |
| Community | One social space that creates encounter |
| Innovation | One unexpected material or structural move |
| Cultural identity | One local pattern abstracted into form |

### Question 3: Pick your concept emoji
- 🌊 = Flow, horizon, reflection
- 🌳 = Growth, filter, organic
- 🔗 = Connection, bridge, network
- 🌀 = Void, spiral, center
- ⬜ = Frame, grid, order

**Write this down in your phone's notes app. One sentence.**

*Example: "A community library that frames the harbor view through a series of ascending reading terraces" (🌊 + 📚)*

## Minute 9-16: The 3-Board Layout

Use Canva mobile, Picsart, or even Instagram Layout. The goal is **information hierarchy**, not graphic perfection.

### Board 1: The Hook (Hero + Title)
| Element | Placement | Size |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Hero image | Center | 70% of board |
| Project title | Bottom-left | Large, readable |
| Your name | Bottom-right | Small |
| One-line concept | Below title | Medium |

**Title formula:** [Poetic noun] + [Architectural term]
- "Tidal Reading Rooms" (not "Harbor Library")
- "Void Garden Housing" (not "Residential Complex")
- "Echo Chamber Museum" (not "Cultural Center")

### Board 2: The Logic (Concept Diagram)
| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Before/After or Site/Building | Show transformation |
| One analytical diagram | Circulation, light, or program |
| Arrows + 3 labels maximum | Explain the move |

**Don't:** Draw plans. Do: Explain the concept with diagrams.

### Board 3: The Evidence (Plan/Section + Material)
| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Simplified plan or section | Show scale and organization |
| One material detail | Prove constructability |
| Sustainability claim | One number or strategy |

**Plans:** Rough is fine. Show walls, openings, one dimension.
**Sections:** Show vertical relationship (ground to sky, old to new).

## Minute 17-20: Text Polish & Submission

### The 3-Sentence Concept Statement
```
[The problem the site/brief presents] + 
[Your single architectural move] + 
[The effect on users and context]
```

**Example:**
> "The harbor front lacks public spaces for quiet contemplation. A series of ascending timber terraces frame selective water views while creating intimate reading niches. The result is a building that belongs to the sea without dominating it."

### One Technical Highlight
Pick ONE. Don't list five.
- "Modular CLT panels allow 80% off-site fabrication"
- "Rainwater collection system irrigates the rooftop garden"
- "Passive solar shading reduces cooling load by 40%"

### Submission Checklist
- [ ] Hero image (minimum 3000px wide)
- [ ] 3 boards as single PDF or 3 separate images
- [ ] Concept statement (under 100 words)
- [ ] Team names and contact
- [ ] File size under competition limit (usually 10-20MB)

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to mention AI-generated images?**
A: Check competition rules. Most 2026 competitions allow AI concept visuals if labeled. When in doubt, label "Concept visualization generated with AI tools."

**Q: What if the competition requires technical drawings?**
A: Hand-sketch a simplified plan/section. Neat handwriting + one dimension line = acceptable for concept-stage competitions.

**Q: Can I win with a 20-minute entry?**
A: Unlikely for built competitions. Very possible for ideas competitions, student competitions, or conceptual challenges where the idea matters more than the detail.

**Q: What's the biggest mistake in rushed entries?**
A: Trying to do too much. Three clear boards with one idea beats one overcrowded board with five ideas.

**Q: Which competitions are best for quick entries?**
A: Look for: short deadlines (2-4 weeks), conceptual briefs, single-image submissions, student categories.
